    Vegetarian Buddhist restaurant serving Asian and western food with mock meats. Nice and clean. Owners speak basic English and meals are sizable. Buffet served from 11am-2pm daily. The only place outside Vietnam found serving typical western pho style meals, vermicelli with tofu, sprouts, shredded carrots, cucumber, herbs and mock fish sauce, and spring rolls with peanut sauce. Also offers fruit shakes, various seasoned vegetables, and appetizers, soups. Reported closed August 2024.

                        Points +490

                        Vegan

                          Good vegetarian food, cheap, not Buddhist - Edit

                          This is a nice vegetarian restaurant (mostly vegan; I believe they serve dairy in some of the drinks) with very cheap food that comes quick. They have quite a large menu with a lot of options to choose from. I found the fried stuff (like spring rolls) to be a bit oily, but I liked everything else. Good fresh juices as well.

                          They also do a lunch buffet, which is a solid value. But it's good at any time of day. Staff is very attentive and generally kind, but they don't speak much English. The menu does have English, however.

                          I think it's worth clarifying that, contrary to the other reviews and information here, this is not a Buddhist restaurant. It's actually run by a cult, built around a man named Master Ruma. The atmosphere here is very much centered on their leader; when I visited, there was a huge portrait of him on one wall, another wall covered in shelves stuffed with his books and videos, and other images of him scattered throughout. I found this pretty offputting, but still, the staff don't try to directly push it on you in any way.
